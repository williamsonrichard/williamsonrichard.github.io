The characters of the text are presented in the traditional way: in vertical lines (to be read from top to bottom), proceeding from right to left. Punctuation is not included, as it was either not present or only very lightly present in the source manuscripts. Every character is a link to a page for that character. Romanisations in the front matter are according to the Chinese phonetic alphabet (Hanyu Pinyin).

Textual notes

The text is that of Wang Bi's commentary, in the critical version of Rudolf G. Wagner. In particular, its division into sections/chapters and into columns/paragraphs follows this source.

Wagner's text differs from that of the received version of Wang Bi's commentary only in occasional characters. These alterations are carefully argued to likely be more authentic: they come principally from quotations of the text within Wang Bi's commentary itself, where these do not agree with the actual text in the received version, and where they are corroborated by other primary sources, including the most recent textual finds.
